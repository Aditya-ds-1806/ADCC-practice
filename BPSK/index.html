Design of BPSK Receivers and BER Analysis

Experiment - 1

1 Aim

To design a discrete time BPSK communication receiver, and analyze the BER performance.

2 Theory

2.1 ML Rule

Let \(Y\) be the output at the receiver and \(X\) be the message at the transmitter. Let \(N\) be the noise introduced by the AWGN channel. Let \(E\) be the bit energy. Then, for the \(k^{th}\) bit: $$ Y_{k} = X_{k} + N_{k} $$ Consider: $$ P(Y_{k} = y \mid S_{k} = \sqrt{E}) $$ $$ = \lim_{\epsilon \to 0} P(Y_{k} \in (y-\epsilon, y+\epsilon) \mid S_{k}=\sqrt{E}) $$ $$ = \lim_{\epsilon \to 0} P(S_{k} + N_{k} \in (y-\epsilon, y+\epsilon) \mid S_{k}=\sqrt{E}) $$ $$ = \lim_{\epsilon \to 0} P(N_{k} \in (y-\epsilon-\sqrt{E}, y+\epsilon-\sqrt{E}) \mid S_{k}=\sqrt{E}) $$ $$ = \lim_{\epsilon \to 0} 2\epsilon f_{Y_{k}}(y-\sqrt{E}) $$ $$ \boxed{P(Y_{k} = y \mid S_{k} = \sqrt{E}) = \lim_{\epsilon \to 0} 2\epsilon f_{Y_{k}}(y-\sqrt{E})}\tag{1} $$ Similarly: $$ \boxed{P(Y_{k} = y \mid S_{k} = -\sqrt{E}) = \lim_{\epsilon \to 0} 2\epsilon f_{Y_{k}}(y+\sqrt{E})}\tag{2} $$ Consider the ratio of \((1)\) and \((2)\): $$ \frac{P(Y_{k} = y \mid S_{k} = \sqrt{E})}{P(Y_{k} = y \mid S_{k} = -\sqrt{E})} = \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\frac{2\epsilon f_{Y_{k}}(y-\sqrt{E})}{2\epsilon f_{Y_{k}}(y+\sqrt{E})} $$ $$ = \frac{f_{Y_{k}}(y-\sqrt{E})}{f_{Y_{k}}(y+\sqrt{E})} $$ $$ = \frac{\frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2}\pi}e^{-\frac{(y-\sqrt{E})^2}{2\sigma^2}}}{\frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2}\pi}e^{-\frac{(y+\sqrt{E})^2}{2\sigma^2}}} $$ $$ = e^{\frac{(y+\sqrt{E})^2 - (y-\sqrt{E})^2}{2\sigma^2}} $$ $$ = e^{\frac{4y\sqrt{E}}{2\sigma^2}} $$ $$ = e^{\frac{2y\sqrt{E}}{\sigma^2}} $$ $$ \boxed{\frac{P(Y_{k} = y \mid S_{k} = \sqrt{E})}{P(Y_{k} = y \mid S_{k} = -\sqrt{E})} = e^{\frac{2y\sqrt{E}}{\sigma^2}}}\tag{3} $$ Let \(B_{k} \in X \) be the transmitted bit and \(\hat{B} \in Y\) be the the output at the receiver corresponding to \(B_{k}\). Then the ML can be written as: $$ \hat{B} = \begin{cases} 1, & \frac{P(Y_{k}=y \mid B_{k}=1)}{P(Y_{k}=y \mid B_{k}=0)} > 1\\ 0, & \frac{P(Y_{k}=y \mid B_{k}=1)}{P(Y_{k}=y \mid B_{k}=0)} < 1 \end{cases} $$ $$ \hat{B} = \begin{cases} 1, & \ln\Big({\frac{P(Y_{k}=y \mid B_{k}=1)}{P(Y_{k}=y \mid B_{k}=0)}}\Big) > 0\\ 0, & \ln\Big({\frac{P(Y_{k}=y \mid B_{k}=1)}{P(Y_{k}=y \mid B_{k}=0)}}\Big) < 0 \end{cases} $$ Fromt he constellation diagram, we can write: $$ \hat{B} = \begin{cases} 1, & \ln\Big({\frac{P(Y_{k}=y \mid S_{k}=-\sqrt{E})}{P(Y_{k}=y \mid S_{k}=\sqrt{E})}}\Big) > 0\\ 0, & \ln\Big({\frac{P(Y_{k}=y \mid S_{k}=-\sqrt{E})}{P(Y_{k}=y \mid S_{k}=\sqrt{E})}}\Big) < 0 \end{cases}\tag{4} $$ Substituting \((3)\) in \((4)\): $$ \hat{B} = \begin{cases} 1, & \frac{2y\sqrt{E}}{\sigma^2} < 0\\ 0, & \frac{2y\sqrt{E}}{\sigma^2} > 0 \end{cases} $$ $$ \hat{B} = \begin{cases} 1, & y < 0\\ 0, & y > 0 \end{cases} $$

2.2 BER

Let \(E\) be the error bit. Then, $$ P(E=1) = P(E=1, B=1) + P(E=1, B=0) $$ $$ P(E=1) = P(E=1 \mid B=1)P(B=1) + P(E=1 \mid B=0)P(B=0)\tag{5} $$ $$ P(E=1 \mid B=1) = P(\hat{B}=0 \mid B=1) $$ $$ = P(Y>0 \mid B=1) $$ $$ = P(N - \sqrt{E} > 0 \mid B=1) $$ $$ = P(N > \sqrt{E} \mid B=1) $$ $$ = P(N > \sqrt{E}) $$ $$ = P\Big(\frac{N}{\sigma} > \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma^2}}\Big) $$ $$ = \int_{\sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma^2}}}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac{x^2}{2}}dx $$ $$ = Q\Bigg(\sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma^2}}\Bigg) $$ $$ = Q\Bigg(\sqrt{\frac{2E}{N_{0}}}\Bigg)\tag{6} $$ Similarly, $$ P(E=1 \mid B=0) = Q\Bigg(\sqrt{\frac{2E}{N_{0}}}\Bigg)\tag{7} $$ Substituting, \((6)\) and \((7)\) in \((5)\), $$ \boxed{\therefore P_{e} = P(E=1) = Q\Bigg(\sqrt{\frac{2E}{N_{0}}}\Bigg)} $$

3 Design

Input: NO_OF_BITS, EB_N0_DB, Bk
Output: BER

Xk = Bk(0 --> 1, 1 --> -1)
ML(point) = (0 if point > 0; 1 if point < 0)

for EB_N0 in EB_N0_DB
    Nk = AWGN(EB_N0, 1D)
    Yk = Xk + Nk
    bHat = ML(Yk)
    unchangedBits = count(bHat === Bk)
    BER = 1 - (unchangedBits/NO_OF_BITS)

plot(BER, BER_THEORETICAL)
